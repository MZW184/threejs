Lets Dive into My Self-Taught Web Development Journey

In April 2023, I embarked on a self-taught journey into web development. Armed with determination
​and a thirst for knowledge,
I dove headfirst into the vast ocean of code. Here’s how my path unfolded:

HTML, CSS, and JS Beginnings:

I started with the basics: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Creating simple frontend websites
felt like ​a breeze initially, but as time passed, the challenges grew.
Udemy, short free courses, Google, ​and YouTube became my companions though, in hindsight, maybe
not the ideal ones for diving ​into web development.
Despite feeling a bit lost, I refused to give up. I stumbled through the learning process, often ​feeling
like I was wearing a blindfold.

React and ​Terminal ​Adventures:

React soon caught my attention, and I explored the world of npm packages.
The sudden shift in ​programming languages left me feeling disoriented, but I refused to give up.
Even with a metaphorical blindfold on, I persisted.
I sought mentorships and learnerships to ​accelerate my progress, determined to conquer the challenges of
programming, but i was’nt able to ​find any form of support.

Expanding ​Horizons:

To broaden my skill set, I enrolled in a short computer science course. There, I grappled with
C and ​Python, yet I still didn’t feel entirely comfortable as a developer.
I explored game development. Unity and Blender caught my eye, but it was
Unreal Engine that truly sparked my creativity.
C++ blueprints led me to build basic games ,a thrilling experience.
Still, I grappled with self-doubt. Was I truly a developer? ​Where was my creativity
hiding? Tutors remained elusive,
but my determination held ​strong. I wanted to be exceptional, so I kept pushing ​forward.

Finding ​Direction with ​SheCodes:

Recently, I received a reminder from SheCodes about starting my
journey with them in August 2024. ​From scratch, I’ve
immersed myself in their program.

For the first time, I don’t feel like I’m stumbling
blindly; I have a clear direction. SheCodes encourages me to push
my limits, solving problems independently before seeking help.
It’s not that I dislike asking for assistance; rather,
I want to cultivate resilience and self-reliance.
My ultimate goal is to become an exceptional programmer.
I ​intend to continue learning, growing gradually,
and ​contributing meaningfully to projects.
I believe that by ​persistently tackling challenges,
I’ll inch closer to that ​goal—one line of code at a time.
